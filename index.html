Stamped Concrete

Stamped concrete is a decorative finish for freshly poured concrete. Using specialized rubber stamps, patterns are pressed into the surface to create realistic looks of stone, brick, or wood â€” finished with a protective polish for a shiny, premium appearance.

Where it shines:

  • Housing society entrances and walking paths
  • Gates and feature areas with decorative finishes
  • Parks and community spaces
  • Residential driveways, patios, and forecourts
  • Commercial and public walkways

Our Process:

  1. Preparation: Stable base prepared; concrete poured and leveled
  2. Color Application: Base color added using color hardener
  3. Release Agent: Powder or liquid release to prevent sticking
  4. Stamping: Rubber tools pressed to form the texture or design
  5. Jointing: Control joints cut to prevent cracking
  6. Curing & Sealing: Surface cured and sealed for protection
  7. Finishing Polish: Final polish applied for a shiny, elegant look

Khaka Plaster (Base-Coat & Decorative)

Khaka plaster is a sandâ€“cement base coat for walls, pillars, gates, and structures. Unlike stamped concrete, it can be hand-carved into decorative designs and sculptures,making it perfect for housing societies, entrances, parks, and boundary walls.

Where it shines:

  • Gates & Sculptures in housing societies
  • Entrance walkways and park pathways
  • Boundary walls with carved details
  • Residential & commercial buildings

Benefits:

  • Strong, durable base for finishing
  • Custom hand-carved designs
  • Weather-resistant & cost-effective
  • Improves adhesion for paint/coats
